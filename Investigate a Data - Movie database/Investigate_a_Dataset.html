Introduction¶

TMDb movies dataset is a data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), it includes movies infromation, budget, revenue, user ratings, avarage votes and popularity. It is a helpful source of information for analysing the movies' net profit, sucessful genres and there relation with other properties, popularity and revenue of movies. The following question will try to help in analysing and extracting useful insights from the data set.

Net Profit related questions¶

  1. What is the net profit of each movie?
  2. Research Question 2: What is the % of net profit (avg) out of the revenue (avg)?

Genres related questions ¶

  1. What are the Frequent Genres across all movies in the dataset?
  2. What are the Top 3 Genres that achieve the Highest Profit each year?
  3. What are the Top 10 Genres with the highest profit across all movies over all the years found in the dataset?
  4. What are the populare genres over each year (top 3 genres for each year)?
  5. Which genres are most popular from year to year?

Popularity related questions¶

  1. What is the correlation between revenue and popularity, budget, vote count, vote average, runtime, release year?
  2. what is the correlation between vote counts, average votes and popularity?

Revenue related questions ¶

  1. How many movies achieve high revenue, where 'high revenue' is revenue higher than the average of all?
  2. What are the Top 5 movies in terms of revenue?
  3. What kinds of properties are associated with movies that have high revenue?

from IPython.core.display import display, HTML
from base64 import b64encode
from io import BytesIO


def printdf(dfs, titles=None):
    '''
    Print pandas DataFrames side-by-side.

            Parameters:
                    dfs (list or DataFrame): list of DataFrames or sigle DataFrame
                    titles (list): list of strings acts as caption for each dataframe respectively

    '''
    dfs = [dfs] if (type(dfs)!=list) else dfs
    titles = [titles] if (type(titles)==str) else titles
    titles = ["dataframe_" + str(i+1) for i in range(len(dfs))] if titles==None else titles 
    output = ""
    for title, df in dict(zip(titles, dfs)).items():
        output += df.style.set_table_attributes("style='display:inline;margin-right:20px;'").set_caption(title)._repr_html_() + "\xa0\xa0\xa0"
    display(HTML(output))
    

def showplotdf(first, second, title=None):
    '''
    Print pandas DataFrames and figure/plot side-by-side.

            Parameters:
                    first (DataFrame or figure or plot): to be printed on the left side 
                    second (DataFrame or figure or plot): to be printed on the right side 

    '''
    def _identify(obj):
        obj = obj._repr_html_() if isinstance(obj, pd.DataFrame) else obj
        
        if str(type(obj)) =="<class 'matplotlib.axes._subplots.AxesSubplot'>" or type(obj)==plt.Figure:
            obj = obj.get_figure() if type(obj)!=plt.Figure else obj
            figdata = BytesIO()
            #plt.tight_layout()
            obj.savefig(figdata, format='png', bbox_inches = "tight")
            plt.close()
            obj =  f'<img src="data:image/png;base64,{b64encode(figdata.getvalue()).decode()}"/>'
        
        return obj
    
    caption = f'<caption style="color:white;background-color:#999;padding:5px;text-align:center">{title}</caption>' if title != None else ''
    width = f'width:100%'
    second = _identify(second)
    first = _identify(first)
    
    datatable = (f"""<table style="{width}">
                        {caption}
                        <tr style="background-color: transparent;vertical-align: top;">
                            <td style="background-color: transparent;vertical-align: top;text-align: left;">{first}</td>
                            <td style="background-color: transparent;vertical-align: top;text-align: left;">{second}</td>
                        </tr>
                    </table>""")
    display(HTML("<style>div.output_scroll { height: auto; }</style>"))
    display(HTML(datatable))

Conclusions¶

Analysis results¶

According to the above analysis, we have come across different facts about movies profit, genres and revenue. the following are some of the insights.

  • Average profit of the movies is: 71,178,964
  • Movies achieved highest profit (Top 5): Avatar, Star Wars: The Force Awakens, Titanic, Jurassic World and Furious 7

  • Average profit for movies (5 movies sample): The Cell, Vanishing Point, 2 Guns, The Possession and Blood Diamond

  • The percentage of average net profit out of the average revenue (considering all movies) is: 65.5%

  • Frequent Genres across all movies (Top 5): Drama, Comedy, Thriller, Action, and Adventure

  • Genres that achieve the Highest Profit in each year (Top 3 for 2015 as an example): Action, Adventure, Science Fiction
  • Genres with the highest profit across all movies over all the years (Top 3): Adventure = 111,126,445,901, Action = 108,558,430,318 , Comedy = 87,374,955,013
  • Genres that are gaining popularity year after year are: Adventure, Action and Science Fiction

Looking at the relation between the movies properties, it is clear that:

  • There is a positive correlation between revenue and popularity, budget, vote_count
  • There is also a positive correlation between popularity and vote_count

From the revenue perspective, there are 1096 movies achieve revenue more then the average revenue (108,640,982). Digging in to the value, the Top 5 Movies with the highest revenue are:

  • Avatar 2,781,505,847
  • Star Wars (The Force Awakens) 2,068,178,225
  • Titanic 1,845,034,188
  • The Avengers 1,519,557,910
  • Jurassic World 1,513,52,8810

Finally, to produce a movie that would achieve high revenue, it seems that the following criteria may guarantee hight revenue:

  • Average budget should be around 74,460,416
  • Director might be one of the top 5 : Steven Spielberg, Robert Zemeckis, Michael Bay, Ridley Scott, Tony Scott
  • Cast might include one or more of the top 5: Tom Cruise, Brad Pitt, Tom Hanks, Sylvester Stallone, Bruce Willis
  • Production company might be one of the top 5: Universal Pictures, Warner Bros., Paramount Pictures, Twentieth Century Fox Film Corporation, Columbia Pictures
  • Average runtime should be around 115 min

Assumptions & Limitations

  • To get movies with high revenue, high revenue threshold is set to the revenue above the average revenue of all movies.
  • There are some movies small budget and/or revenue, to make the analysis realistic threshold of 1000 has been used to discard movies with budget and/or revenue less than that threshold.
